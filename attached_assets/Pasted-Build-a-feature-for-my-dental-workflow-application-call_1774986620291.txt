Build a feature for my dental workflow application called the “Predictive Nudge & Follow-Up System.”

The goal is to use predictive analytics and workflow logic to determine when a patient should ideally return for their next appointment, based on both historical behavior and clinical treatment progression.

Core Feature Requirements

1. Predictive Appointment Recommendation
Create a predictive analytics engine that recommends a patient’s next appointment date and time using:
- The patient’s historical scheduling behavior
- The patient’s attendance patterns
- The day/time they most often accept or complete appointments
- The treatment that was most recently completed
- The expected clinical follow-up timeline tied to the treatment phase

Example:
If a patient historically prefers Friday appointments around 9:00 AM, the system should detect that pattern and prioritize recommending Friday at 9:00 AM for future appointments.

The model should recognize patterns such as:
- Preferred weekday
- Preferred time of day
- Frequency of cancellations or reschedules
- Time gaps between visits
- Likelihood of returning within a recommended treatment window

2. Treatment-Based Follow-Up Logic
The predictive system should also determine when a patient needs a follow-up appointment based on the treatment completed and the expected dental treatment phase timeline.

Use the following treatment phase framework:

Phase 0: Assessment & Diagnosis
- Description: Comprehensive examination, X-rays, and medical/dental history review
- Timeline: Immediate, usually 45–60 minutes

Phase I: Acute/Urgent Care
- Description: Pain relief, emergency extractions, temporary fillings
- Timeline: 0–2 weeks
- Goal: Stabilize the patient and resolve immediate problems

Phase II: Disease Control & Stabilization
- Description: Scaling and root planing, caries removal, oral hygiene education
- Timeline: 2–6 weeks
- Initial therapy may span 2–4 weeks

Phase III: Re-evaluation
- Description: Reassess tissue response to periodontal therapy and healing
- Timeline: 4–6 weeks after Phase II
- Goal: Confirm periodontal stability before irreversible restorative treatment begins

Phase IV: Definitive / Restorative Phase
- Description: Final restorations, crowns, bridges, implants, orthodontics
- Timeline: 2–3 months or longer depending on case complexity

Phase V: Maintenance & Prevention
- Description: Regular recall visits, cleanings, and monitoring
- Timeline: Every 3–6 months based on patient need

The system should generate follow-up recommendations based on these phases.
Examples:
- After scaling and root planing, recommend periodontal re-evaluation in 4–6 weeks
- After urgent care, recommend short-term follow-up within 1–2 weeks if needed
- After definitive restorative treatment, recommend appropriate restorative review or maintenance
- For maintenance patients, recommend 3-month, 4-month, or 6-month recall intervals based on patient history and risk

3. Compliance and Case Progression Rules
The workflow should account for:
- Case complexity
- Patient compliance
- Whether the patient completed disease control before moving into restorative treatment
- Whether treatment progression should pause if the patient is non-compliant

Example:
If the patient fails disease control or oral hygiene improvement, the system should flag that treatment progression may need to stop before advancing to definitive restorative care.

4. Nudge System
Add an automated nudge system for both providers and scheduling staff.

The system should generate nudges for:
- Patients overdue for follow-up
- Patients due soon based on phase logic
- Patients who historically delay care
- Patients in specialty treatment with missing updates
- Students/providers who need to document next steps
- Cases approaching graduation transfer deadlines

Nudges should appear as:
- Dashboard alerts
- Chart notifications
- Task reminders
- Suggested next action messages

Examples:
- “Patient is due for periodontal re-evaluation this week.”
- “Patient historically books Friday mornings. Recommend next visit on Friday at 9:00 AM.”
- “Patient referred to specialty care; specialty status update needed.”
- “Student graduation approaching. Transfer roster assignment required.”

5. Specialty Referral Tracking
Add a workflow for patients referred to specialty treatment.

Requirements:
- A status field showing whether the patient is in specialty care
- A field to input the referral date to specialty treatment
- A shared-view option so postgraduate/specialty providers can view relevant patient workflow information
- A status tracker for specialty progress
- Alerts if a specialty referral is open without updated treatment documentation

6. Faculty Documentation
In the patient chart, include an input field to record the name of the faculty member who worked with the student on the day of the procedure.

Requirements:
- Faculty name field tied to each visit or procedure entry
- Searchable in reporting
- Included in workflow history and notes

7. D4 Graduation Transfer Workflow
Create a roster transfer feature for graduating D4 students.

Requirements:
- When treatment is marked as completed OR when the student’s graduation date is reached, a pop-up should appear
- The pop-up should ask the student who they want to transfer the patient roster to
- All relevant patient notes, workflow history, and care status should transfer to the assigned student/provider
- The system should maintain an audit trail of the transfer
- The transfer workflow should prevent patients from being left unassigned

Example pop-up:
“Graduation or treatment completion detected. Please assign this patient roster to the next student/provider.”

8. Suggested Data Model
Design tables or models for:
- Patients
- Appointments
- Appointment history
- Treatment phases
- Procedures completed
- Follow-up rules
- Predictive recommendations
- Nudges / alerts
- Specialty referrals
- Faculty documentation
- Student roster transfers
- Providers / students / postgraduate providers

9. UI / Workflow Expectations
Create a clean workflow inside the patient chart that includes:
- Next recommended appointment
- Reason for recommendation
- Treatment phase status
- Specialty referral status
- Faculty name entry
- Nudge alerts
- Transfer roster action when applicable

10. Analytics / Logic Expectations
Use rules-based logic first, with a structure that can later support machine learning.
The system should:
- Analyze prior appointment patterns
- Recommend the most likely accepted appointment slot
- Estimate when follow-up care is clinically due
- Flag overdue patients
- Support explainable recommendations, not black-box results

Examples of explainable recommendation text:
- “Recommended because patient attended 4 of last 5 appointments on Fridays between 8:30 AM and 10:00 AM.”
- “Recommended because Phase III re-evaluation is due 4 weeks after SRP completion.”
- “Maintenance recall suggested at 3 months due to periodontal history and prior treatment pattern.”

- Use clean architecture
- Make it scalable
- Include sample seed data
- Include comments in code
- Include a simple dashboard or chart view for nudges and predicted next appointments
- Prefer an MVP that works end-to-end, even if the predictive model begins with rules and weighted scoring instead of advanced machine learning

4. Frontend workflow
